New Computer Science Department Chair Announced

Academic | October 10, 2023

Back to Listing
Edit Delete
Academic Faculty News Computer Science
324 views

The University has announced the appointment of Dr. Sarah Johnson as the new Chair of the Computer Science Department, effective November 1st, 2023.

Dr. Johnson, a distinguished professor and researcher in the field of artificial intelligence and machine learning, will succeed Dr. Robert Chen, who has served as Department Chair for the past six years.

University President Dr. Maria Rodriguez expressed her enthusiasm for the appointment: "Dr. Johnson brings exceptional leadership skills and a visionary approach to computer science education. Her extensive research background and industry connections will help strengthen our department's position as a leader in computer science education and research."

Dr. Johnson joined the university faculty in 2015 after a successful career at Tech Innovations Inc., where she led the AI research division. She holds a Ph.D. in Computer Science from Stanford University and has published over 50 research papers in top-tier journals and conferences.

"I am honored to take on this role and excited to work with our talented faculty, staff, and students to continue building a forward-thinking computer science program. My vision is to enhance our curriculum with more practical, industry-relevant projects while maintaining our strong theoretical foundation."

Dr. Sarah Johnson

New Initiatives Planned

Under Dr. Johnson's leadership, the Computer Science Department plans to launch several new initiatives, including:

  • An expanded internship program with leading tech companies
  • A new interdisciplinary AI and Ethics certificate program
  • Enhanced research opportunities for undergraduate students
  • A mentorship program connecting students with industry professionals

The department will also undergo facility renovations, with plans to modernize computer labs and create collaborative workspace areas for student projects.

Student Reaction

The announcement has been met with enthusiasm from computer science students. Mia Chen, president of the Computer Science Student Association, stated, "We're excited about Dr. Johnson's appointment. She's been a fantastic professor and mentor, and her industry experience will bring valuable real-world perspectives to our education."

The Computer Science Department will host a welcome reception for Dr. Johnson on November 3rd in the Science Building atrium from 3:00 to 5:00 PM. All students, faculty, and staff are invited to attend.
